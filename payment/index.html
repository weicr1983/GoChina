Payment

Pay like a local in 5 minutes

The biggest change for foreign visitors since 2024: you can now bind a Visa or Mastercard to Alipay and WeChat Pay. Cash, ATMs, and cards still work too.

Alipay (支付宝)

Bind your Visa / Mastercard / Amex / JCB in the app. Works at virtually every shop, restaurant, metro, taxi, and attraction in China.

Recommended

How to set it up

  1. 01 Download Alipay (search 'Alipay' in your app store, or use the international version).
  2. 02 Open the app, tap 'Services' or 'Me → Bank Cards / Add Card'.
  3. 03 Enter your card details. Alipay verifies with a small charge (~$0.50) that refunds in 1–3 days.
  4. 04 Set a 6-digit payment password. You're done — start scanning merchant QR codes.
Limits
Single transaction: ~$1,000 (without extra verification). Annual: $50,000. Above that, upload a passport photo to raise limits.
Fees
No fee from Alipay. Your bank may charge a 1–3% foreign transaction fee.

WeChat Pay (微信支付)

Same flow as Alipay, integrated inside WeChat. Many Chinese merchants prefer it, and you'll need it for in-person social situations.

Also recommended

How to set it up

  1. 01 Open WeChat → Me → Services → Wallet → Bank Cards.
  2. 02 Add a Visa / Mastercard. Verification takes under 2 minutes.
  3. 03 Set payment password. Pay by scanning or showing your QR code to be scanned.
Limits
Similar to Alipay. Limits raise after passport upload.
Fees
1–3% foreign transaction fee from your card issuer.

Cash (RMB)

Carry small amounts (¥200–500) for street food, rural areas, or as a backup. Note that some small vendors no longer accept cash.

Still works

How to use it

  1. 01 Withdraw from any major bank ATM using Visa / Mastercard / UnionPay / Cirrus / Plus.
  2. 02 Bank of China, ICBC, and China Construction Bank ATMs have English menus.
  3. 03 Daily ATM limit: ¥2,500 per card. Per-transaction fee: typically ¥10–30 + your bank's foreign fee.
Limits
None, beyond ATM caps.
Fees
¥10–30 domestic fee + 1–3% from your card issuer.

Bank card (Visa / Mastercard)

Hotels, large restaurants, and most big-box stores accept foreign cards. Smaller shops, taxis, and street vendors usually do not.

Variable

How to use it

  1. 01 Always carry a backup Visa or Mastercard.
  2. 02 Notify your bank of travel to avoid anti-fraud blocks.
  3. 03 Expect 1–3% foreign transaction fee on every charge.
Limits
Depends on your bank.
Fees
1–3% foreign transaction fee.

Two tips that save real money

  • — Bind your card to BOTH Alipay and WeChat before you leave home. Some merchants only accept one. Setting up takes 5 minutes; troubleshooting in a Beijing hutong takes an hour.
  • — Always choose to pay in the LOCAL currency (CNY) when offered. 'Pay in your home currency' (Dynamic Currency Conversion) usually costs an extra 3–5%.
