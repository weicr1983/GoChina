About

Why we built this

China's 144 / 240-hour visa-free transit policy is one of the most generous short-stay schemes in the world. But the official information is fragmented, mostly in Chinese, and assumes you already know things like how to bind a foreign card to Alipay, or which airports actually accept transit permits.

We built GoChina Trip to answer the four questions every transit traveler actually asks: how do I come, how do I pay, how do I move, and how much does it cost. Not with long blog posts, but with tools you can use in two minutes.

What you'll find here

  • — Visa guide — 144 vs 240 side-by-side, eligible ports, documents, airport procedure.
  • — Budget calculator — per-city breakdown in USD and CNY. Share the URL with your travel partner.
  • — Itinerary planner — coming in V1. Pick your hours and interests, get a route.
  • — City pages — what to do, how to get in, what it costs.

How we make money

The site is free. We are supported by Google AdSense ads and may include affiliate links to booking platforms. We never sell tours, and we never take commissions from hotels or attractions. Editorial recommendations are independent.

Editorial standards

We verify all visa-related information against the National Immigration Administration of China and update the site when policies change. Prices in the budget calculator are sampled quarterly from Booking, Trip.com, and 12306. If you spot an error, please tell us.
