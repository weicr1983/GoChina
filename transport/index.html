Transport

Getting around China

Public transport in China is fast, cheap, foreigner-friendly, and doesn't require a Chinese phone number. Here's what you actually use.

High-speed rail (高铁)

The backbone of intercity travel. Up to 350 km/h, English announcements, e-tickets via passport — no paper ticket needed.

HSR

How to use it

  1. 01 Book on 12306.cn (English version) or via the Trip.com app. Avoid third-party resellers charging extra.
  2. 02 Enter your passport number exactly as printed. The name must match.
  3. 03 At the station, scan your passport at the gate — no paper ticket. Foreign passports are accepted at all major stations.
Apps
12306 (official), Trip.com
Payment
Alipay, WeChat Pay, or Visa / Mastercard via 12306

Metro (地铁)

In every major city. Signs are bilingual (Chinese + English). Single-journey tickets from machines; daily caps apply.

M

How to use it

  1. 01 Buy a single ticket from the green vending machines (English menu available). Pay cash or scan Alipay / WeChat at the QR reader.
  2. 02 For multi-day stays, buy a rechargeable transport card at the service counter (e.g. Shanghai Public Transportation Card, Beijing Yikatong).
  3. 03 Tap in and tap out. Daily cap means you never pay more than ~¥18/day in any city.
Apps
Each city has its own metro app, but Alipay's 'Metro' mini-program covers all major cities.
Payment
Cash, Alipay, WeChat Pay, or transport card

DiDi (滴滴)

China's Uber. Set pickup, see fare estimate, pay in-app. Works without a Chinese phone number if you bind a Visa / Mastercard.

Car

How to use it

  1. 01 Download DiDi from the App Store or Google Play.
  2. 02 Sign up with your foreign phone number (Gmail works for email).
  3. 03 Add a Visa or Mastercard to the wallet.
  4. 04 Type destination in English — the driver sees it in Chinese. Use the in-app chat for clarification.
Apps
DiDi (international version)
Payment
Visa, Mastercard, Alipay, WeChat Pay

Bike-share (共享单车)

Yellow Meituan and blue HelloRide bikes are everywhere. Scan the QR code with Alipay — no app install needed.

Bike

How to use it

  1. 01 Open Alipay, tap 'Scan', and point at the bike's QR code.
  2. 02 First ride: ¥1.5 ($0.20) for 30 min. After: ¥1.5 per 30 min.
  3. 03 Park anywhere on a sidewalk, ending the ride in the app.
Apps
Meituan, HelloRide, or just Alipay mini-program
Payment
Alipay, WeChat Pay

Taxi (出租车)

Metered, cheap by Western standards, but cash-only. Useful when DiDi can't find a driver.

Taxi

How to use it

  1. 01 Flag a green or red-and-green taxi on the street, or queue at a hotel / station taxi stand.
  2. 02 Show the driver your destination in Chinese characters (use a translation app or a screenshot).
  3. 03 Pay in cash or ask to scan a QR code (most modern taxis accept Alipay / WeChat).
  4. 04 Tip: not expected. Round up if you like.
Apps
DiDi
Payment
Cash, Alipay, WeChat Pay

Apps to install before you fly

Alipay

Pays everywhere. Also unlocks bike-share, metro, DiDi.

WeChat

Pays + communicates. Hotels and many small shops prefer it.

DiDi

Ride-hailing in every city.

Trip.com

Easiest 12306 booking for foreigners. English. Visa / Mastercard.

Maps.me or Amap

Amap (高德地图) is best in China. Maps.me works offline for cities you've pre-downloaded.

Pleco

Best Chinese dictionary. Offline. Camera-OCR for menus and signs.

Internet & SIM

  • — China blocks Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook, X, Reddit, most news sites, and many VPNs. Plan ahead.
  • — Buy a SIM at the airport on arrival: China Unicom has the best international roaming. Or rent a pocket WiFi at the airport for ~¥30/day.
  • — Install a VPN BEFORE you arrive. Even with roaming, most Western apps are inaccessible without one. ExpressVPN, Astrill, and LetsVPN are commonly used.
  • — Hotel WiFi and most cafe WiFi also require a Chinese phone number to receive a one-time SMS code. Plan B: eSIM with China data.
